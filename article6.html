British Army Trains Ukrainian Forces to Safeguard Critical Infrastructure

By Thomas Hughes

03/03/2024






In a bid to fortify Ukraine against Russian aggression, British Army Royal Engineer specialists have initiated a groundbreaking training program aimed at enhancing Ukraine's capacity to defend its critical national infrastructure (CNI) from potential Russian assaults. This effort, crucial in light of Putin's unlawful invasion, targets the preservation of civilian lives and vital infrastructure during the impending winter months.

The threat posed by Russian missile and drone strikes looms large over Ukrainian civilians, making the protection of CNI a top priority. The specialized training provided by British experts equips Ukrainian forces with essential skills to identify vulnerable points within their infrastructure, assess blast radius, and strategize the placement of physical and aerial defenses to thwart Russian attacks.

Defence Secretary Grant Shapps emphasized the urgency of support for Ukraine amidst continuous peril from Russian forces. The tailored training regimen, tailored to Ukraine's specific needs, is anticipated to play a pivotal role in mitigating risks and safeguarding lives during the harsh winter season, where access to reliable energy is paramount.

Developed in response to a direct request from Ukraine, the two-week training program signifies a significant step forward in bolstering the country's energy sector resilience. It builds upon previous courses, progressing from fundamental protective measures to more advanced strategies tailored to the evolving threat landscape.

Recent announcements by Defence Secretary Grant Shapps underscore the UK's commitment to supporting Ukraine, including a £100 million capability package procured through the International Fund for Ukraine. Moreover, the UK-hosted Ukraine Recovery Conference heralded additional assistance, including a £45 million funding boost for Ukraine's energy sector recovery efforts.

The collaborative nature of the training, bringing together experts from various sectors including energy, water, transport, and academia, fosters knowledge-sharing and strengthens the resilience of Ukraine's CNI network. Major Michael Suddaby of 63 Works Group Royal Engineers expressed confidence in the training's real-world impact, highlighting the dedication of Ukrainian participants and their ability to apply acquired skills to protect their nation.

The involvement of specialist reservists, drawing upon their professional expertise from the UK's own CNI sector, underscores the comprehensive approach adopted in the training. Drawing on experiences from disaster relief missions in Bangladesh and Nepal, the course provides Ukrainian participants with practical solutions and scenario-based training to fortify their CNI facilities against potential threats.

As tensions persist in the region, the British Army's collaborative efforts with Ukraine stand as a testament to international solidarity and support in the face of adversity.
